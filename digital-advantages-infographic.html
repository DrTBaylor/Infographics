Regeneration: The Killer Feature

Why digital wins for long-distance communication

The fundamental problem: Every amplifier adds noise. In analog systems, that noise accumulates with each hop. After many repeaters, the signal drowns in accumulated noise.

Digital's solution: At each repeater, we don't amplify ‚Äî we regenerate. We ask: "Is this a 0 or a 1?" and transmit a fresh, clean signal. The noise doesn't accumulate.

Real-world impact: This is why intercontinental fiber optic cables work. A photon traveling 10,000 km would be completely lost without digital regeneration at each repeater station.
